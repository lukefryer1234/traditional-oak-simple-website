Add Authorized Domains to Firebase Authentication

Important: You need to manually add authorized domains to your Firebase Authentication settings to fix the "auth/api-key-is-not-valid" error.

Step 1: Go to Firebase Authentication Settings

Click the link below to open the Firebase Authentication settings for your project:

Open Firebase Authentication Settings

Step 2: Add the Following Domains

In the "Authorized domains" section, make sure the following domains are added:

localhost timberline-commerce.web.app timberline-commerce.firebaseapp.com

Click the "Add domain" button for each domain that isn't already in the list.

Step 3: Save Changes

After adding the domains, make sure to save your changes.

Step 4: Clear Browser Cache

To ensure the changes take effect:

  1. Open your browser's Developer Tools (F12 or right-click â†’ Inspect)
  2. Go to the "Application" tab
  3. Select "Storage" or "Clear storage" on the left
  4. Check all boxes and click "Clear site data"
  5. Close and reopen your browser

Step 5: Return to Your Application

Return to your application and try signing in again. The "auth/api-key-is-not-valid" error should be resolved.

Note: These settings typically take effect immediately, but can sometimes take a few minutes to propagate.
